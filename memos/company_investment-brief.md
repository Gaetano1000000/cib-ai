- **Geography**: Primarily focused on the European market, with plans for expansion into North America.
- **Revenue logic + take rate**: Revenue generated through transaction fees at a take rate of 2.5% on processed payments (Assumption: High confidence).
- **Scale**: Currently has 100,000 users and 5,000 merchants, with projected transaction volume of $1B annually (Assumption: Medium confidence); this implies an annual revenue of $25M.
- **Strongest signals**: Recently secured $10M in Series A funding (Source 1) and expanded partnerships with two major e-commerce platforms (Source 2).
- **Structural advantage**: Proprietary technology that enhances transaction speed and security.
- **Structural risk**: High competition from established players like PayPal and Stripe, which may limit market penetration.

Clear stance: Needs Deeper Diligence

# 2) Business Model & Monetization Logic
- **How money is made**: Revenue is generated through transaction fees charged to merchants for each payment processed.
- **Take rate logic**: The 2.5% take rate is applied to each transaction, with potential for tiered pricing based on volume (Assumption: Medium confidence).
- **Unit economics sensitivity**: A drop in transaction volume below $500M annually would make the business model unsustainable due to fixed costs (Assumption: High confidence).
- **Operating leverage vs fixed compliance cost base**: High fixed compliance costs due to regulatory requirements in the payments industry; operating leverage is limited until scale is achieved.
